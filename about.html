// 01 — about

Who we are.

A small group of volunteers in Birmingham who think technology should work for everyone — including the people it usually leaves behind.

// 02 — the idea

Where we started.

Software gets built for the people who already have it easy. Community groups make do with tools that were not designed for them. New developers from this city graduate with strong skills and no way to prove them. And plenty of people just want to feel less lost when their phone updates.

We think a small group of people, working in the open, can chip away at all three. That is what Birmingham Tech Collective is.

We are forming as a community interest company — a not-for-profit structure that locks our purpose into our articles. Any surplus goes back into the work, not out to shareholders.

// 03 — principles

  • // open.md

    Everything in the open

    Our code is open source. Our decisions are documented. We do not build behind closed doors or make promises without showing our working.

  • // honest.md

    Honest about what we are

    We are still forming. We are small. We are not taking money or making commitments we cannot keep yet. We think honesty about that is more useful than a polished pitch.

  • // community.md

    With the community, not for it

    We build with the people who will use the software, not just for them. That means slower decisions sometimes. It means better ones more often.
