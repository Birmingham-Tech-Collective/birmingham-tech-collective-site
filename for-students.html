// 01 — for students & new developers

For students and new developers.

If you’re a student, recent graduate, or career-changer in Birmingham, this is a real way to get real experience.

// 02 — what you get

What you get

  • Real open-source code on a live project.
  • Structured code review and mentorship from working engineers.
  • Bursaries where we have funding — and we’ll say plainly when we don’t.
  • Credit in commits, documentation, and references.
  • A portfolio piece that actually shipped.

// 04 — what we ask

What we ask

  • A small, honest time commitment.
  • Conduct that matches our community values.
  • Willingness to ship work that prioritises accessibility, not just personal preference.

// 05 — final-year project briefs

Final-year project briefs

We’re working with course leaders at Birmingham City University, Aston University, and the University of Birmingham to define final-year project briefs that align with our roadmap. If you’re a student, ask your course leader. If you’re a course leader, the next section is for you.

// 06 — for course leaders

For course leaders

If you’d like to scope a brief with us, get in touch. We’re happy to talk through what would fit your module and our roadmap. Email hello@birminghamtech.collective and we’ll take it from there.
