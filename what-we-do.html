// 01 — what we do

Three things, done properly.

We are a small group of volunteers forming as a community interest company in Birmingham. Everything we do is in the open.

  • // platform.ts

    Software for the community

    We design and build tools with community organisations in Birmingham — starting with a community events platform. Accessibility is baked in from the first commit, not bolted on at the end. We work with the people who will use the software, not just for them.

    Read about the platform →

  • // students.md

    A way in for new developers

    Real open-source experience, mentorship, and credit for the work. We partner with Birmingham City University, Aston, and the University of Birmingham on final-year project briefs. If you are a student or a course leader, we want to hear from you.

    For students & course leaders →

  • // literacy.org

    Tech literacy, plainly explained

    Short, no-jargon sessions for people who feel left behind by everyday technology. We currently work with a deaf community group in Birmingham. If you run a community organisation and think this could help, get in touch.

    Talk to us →

// 02 — how we work

How we work.

Everything is open source. We do not take on work we cannot do well. We do not promise things we cannot deliver. We move slowly and carefully, because the communities we work with deserve that.

Right now we are a founder and a small group of volunteers. We are forming as a Community Interest Company so that we can take funding, be accountable to a board, and have a legal structure that puts the mission first.
